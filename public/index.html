How I Built This Bot

This started as a question: can I build a trading bot that actually makes money? Not a tutorial project, not a toy — a real system that watches real markets and makes real decisions.

What I ended up building — accidentally — is a systematic algorithmic trading system driven entirely by a financial AI model. No emotional decisions, no gut feelings. Every entry, exit, and position size is generated by a pre-trained model and enforced by hard rules.

The Journey

I didn't start here. I started with "can we just guess up or down every five minutes?" — a simple momentum voter on 5-minute candles. Three signals vote, and if two agree, bet. It sounded clean. It was also completely useless: 10% win rate, negative EV, a max loss streak of 51. Crypto on short timeframes is noise, and fees eat everything.

So I moved to 15-minute candles and tried mean reversion — buy when price drops below the Bollinger Band, sell when it bounces back. Better idea, but 15% win rate. Price would touch the band and keep going. Catching falling knives.

Adding a confirmation candle took that from 15% to 57%. But during that work, a more interesting question came up: what if instead of using AI as a filter on top of technical rules, we replaced the technical rules entirely with AI?

How It Trades Now (v7 — Kronos Direct)

The bot monitors BTC, ETH, and SOL on 15-minute candles, 24 hours a day. On every candle close, it sends the last 96 candles (24 hours of OHLCV data) to a locally-running AI model called Kronos.

Kronos returns two things: a direction (UP or DOWN) and a confidence score. If confidence is ≥65%, the bot enters a trade — but only if the forecast also clears a minimum 1.5:1 risk/reward threshold. No regime detection, no Bollinger Bands, no RSI. The AI is the signal, and the math has to make sense.

  • Entry — market order at close of the triggering candle
  • Stop loss — 1.5× ATR below entry (LONG) or above (SHORT). Adapts to volatility.
  • Target — derived from Kronos's own 5-candle price forecast (~75 min). Must offer ≥1.5× the stop distance.
  • Breakeven stop — once 0.5× ATR in profit, stop moves to entry. Zero risk from that point.
  • Time exit — if still open after 20 bars (~5 hours), exit regardless

What the Data Is Showing

Live paper trading revealed something unexpected: Kronos's all-time directional accuracy was sitting at 23% — well below the 50% you'd expect from random guessing. That number is both a problem and a clue.

A model that's consistently wrong isn't useless. If the error is systematic, inverting every signal should theoretically yield the mirror outcome — in this case, ~77% win rate. The 🔄 Kronos Inverted backtest on this dashboard tests exactly that hypothesis against 30 days of real price data. If it holds, it becomes the next version of the strategy.

Three changes were deployed to stabilise the current mode while that's being tested:

  • Confidence threshold raised 52% → 65% — fewer trades, only high-conviction calls
  • Minimum 1.5:1 R:R guard added — skips entries where the forecast target doesn't justify the stop
  • Breakeven stop corrected 1.5× → 0.5× ATR — was mistakenly cutting winners before they could run

Why Kronos?

Kronos is a financial foundation model accepted at AAAI 2026, pre-trained on K-line data from over 45 global exchanges. Unlike general-purpose time-series models, Kronos processes full OHLCV candlestick sequences using a decoder-only Transformer architecture, the same class of model behind large language models.

Traditional technical indicators (RSI, Bollinger Bands, ADX) are backward-looking — they describe what already happened. Kronos produces a forward-looking probability estimate: given everything it has seen across 45 exchanges, what does this candlestick sequence suggest will happen next?

Position Sizing: The Kelly Connection

Every trade bets 10% of the current portfolio value, not a fixed dollar amount. When you win, the next bet is bigger. When you lose, it shrinks. This is straight out of the Kelly Criterion playbook — adopted by hedge funds including Renaissance Technologies and Bridgewater Associates.

Safety Nets

  • $95 hard floor — trading pauses if the portfolio drops below $95
  • 1.5:1 minimum R:R — no entry unless the potential reward justifies the risk
  • Breakeven stop — once 0.5× ATR in profit, stop moves to entry. Zero risk from that point.
  • Max 3 positions — BTC, ETH, and SOL move together. Caps total correlated exposure.
  • 5-min cooldown — prevents re-entering the same coin immediately after a trade
  • 5% daily loss limit — stops trading until tomorrow
  • 40% kill switch — permanent shutdown if cumulative losses hit 40%
  • Kronos halt — if the AI service goes offline, the bot holds. It does not fall back to guessing.

🎯 Polymarket Arb

Prediction markets are strange. You can buy YES and NO on the same question, and if the two prices don’t add up to exactly $1, there’s free money on the table — in theory. This scanner checks over 600 markets on Polymarket every 30 seconds looking for exactly that. I call it a gap.

I filter it down to markets with at least $5,000 in volume, because thin markets can show prices that look wrong but can’t actually be filled. Any gap above 2 cents on a liquid market gets paper-traded automatically. So far the gaps exist but they’re small — the market is surprisingly efficient. I’m mainly running this to see if anything meaningful ever shows up.

💰 Funding Rate Arbitrage

Crypto perpetual futures charge a “funding rate” every 8 hours — basically, longs pay shorts or vice versa depending on which way the crowd is leaning. When this rate gets extreme, it means one side is so overcrowded they’re paying a premium just to hold their position.

The idea is simple: when funding is extreme, go delta-neutral — buy spot and short the perp simultaneously — and just collect the payment every 8 hours without taking any directional risk. I’ve set the trigger at 0.03% per 8h (roughly 3× the normal rate). This one might sit idle for days, but when it fires it should be a clean collect. Paper balance $100, 25% sizing.

📈 Fear & Greed Contrarian

The Crypto Fear & Greed Index runs from 0 (pure panic) to 100 (pure euphoria). History shows that extreme fear is usually when you should be buying and extreme greed is when you should be selling — basically the opposite of what most people actually do in the moment.

This bot watches the index and buys BTC when it drops below 20 (Extreme Fear), then sells when it crosses back through 80 (Extreme Greed). It’s a slow strategy — maybe 4–6 trades a year — but the logic makes sense to me. The index right now is sitting in Fear territory but not extreme enough to trigger yet. Patience required. 🟡 Stablecoin Depeg Monitor

Stablecoins are supposed to be worth exactly $1. They usually are, but occasionally something spooks the market and USDC or DAI temporarily trades below peg — sometimes as low as $0.96 in a real scare. We saw this with USDC during the SVB bank run in 2023.

When that happens there’s a straightforward trade: buy the depegged stable and wait for it to come back to $1. The risk is it doesn’t, which would mean something seriously broken — but that’s never happened with the major stables. I’m checking USDC and DAI every 2 minutes, and I’ll paper-trade any depeg below $0.995. This might go months without a signal, which is actually fine. Paper balance $100, 25% sizing.

⚡ Liquidation Cascade Scalper

When markets move sharply, over-leveraged traders get liquidated automatically — their positions are force-closed, which often makes the move even more extreme. After a big cascade, the market tends to snap back once the forced selling or buying is done.

This bot looks for moments where a large volume of liquidations hits BTC, ETH, or SOL in a short window, then bets on the mean reversion: buy after a big flush of longs, short after a short squeeze. Target is 1.5% and I give it up to an hour to play out. Note: the raw liquidation feed requires a Binance Futures account, so right now it’s using a large 5-minute price move as a proxy — still valid logic, just slightly less precise. 🔄 Cross-Exchange Spread

The same asset can trade at slightly different prices across different exchanges at the same moment. Professional traders exploit this constantly with fast arb bots — buy cheap on one exchange, sell expensive on the other, pocket the difference. The catch is you need to be very fast and the spreads are tiny.

This is honestly more of a research tool than a live strategy. I compare BTC, ETH, and SOL between Binance and Bybit every 10 seconds to see what the spreads actually look like in practice. Anything above 0.1% gets logged; above 0.5% counts as a paper trade. My gut says realistic spreads will be around 0.01–0.05% which is too small to profit from after fees — but I want to see the real data before writing it off. Paper balance $100, 25% sizing.
