How I Built This Bot

This started as a question: can I build a trading bot that actually makes money? Not a tutorial project, not a toy — a real system that watches real markets and makes real decisions.

What I ended up building — accidentally — is a systematic algorithmic trading system. No emotional decisions, no gut feelings. Every entry, exit, and position size is rule-based and backtestable. The overall style is closest to what the industry calls quantitative trading at the retail level.

The Journey

I didn't start here. I started with "can we just guess up or down every five minutes?" — a simple momentum voter on 5-minute candles. Three signals vote, and if two agree, bet. It sounded clean. It was also completely useless: 10% win rate, negative EV, a max loss streak of 51. Crypto on short timeframes is noise, and fees eat everything.

So I moved to 15-minute candles and tried mean reversion — buy when price drops below the Bollinger Band, sell when it bounces back. Better idea, but 15% win rate. Price would touch the band and keep going. Catching falling knives.

The breakthrough came from a simple rule: don't enter on the touch — wait for the next candle to close back inside the band. One candle of proof that the bounce is real. That single filter tripled the win rate from 15% to 57%. The technique has a name in professional trading: mean reversion with entry confirmation. It's used in serious institutional strategies, not just retail bots.

How It Trades Now (v6)

The bot monitors BTC, ETH, and SOL on 15-minute candles during London and New York sessions. It detects the market regime using ADX and switches between two strategies:

  • Confirmation Candle MR (ranging markets, ADX < 25) — waits for price to close outside the Bollinger Band with extreme RSI, then enters only when the next candle closes back inside. The "bounce-back" is the confirmation.
  • Donchian Breakout (trending markets, ADX > 40) — enters on channel breakouts confirmed by 2× average volume. Trailing stop at 2.5× ATR.

Position Sizing: The Kelly Connection

Every trade bets 3% of the current portfolio value, not a fixed dollar amount. When you win, the next bet is bigger. When you lose, it shrinks. This is straight out of the Kelly Criterion playbook — a formula originally developed for gambling by physicist John Kelly in the 1950s, later adopted by some of the most successful hedge funds in history, including Renaissance Technologies and Bridgewater Associates.

The 3% figure is deliberately conservative — a fractional Kelly — which sacrifices some growth for dramatically lower drawdown risk.

What Was Tested and Rejected

  • MACD confirmation — blocked 100% of mean reversion trades across 30 days. Removed.
  • Momentum voting (5m and 1h) — 10-32% win rate. Pure noise on short timeframes.
  • 1× ATR stops — too tight, caused 87% of trades to stop out before reversing. Loosened to 1.5×.
  • BB touch entry (no confirmation) — 15% win rate. Catching falling knives.

Every decision above came from an 18-combination parameter sweep across 8,640 candles — not intuition.

Safety Nets

  • $95 hard floor — trading pauses and I get a Telegram alert if the portfolio drops 5%
  • Breakeven stop — once 0.5× ATR in profit, the stop moves to the entry price. Zero risk.
  • Max 2 positions — BTC, ETH, and SOL are correlated. No triple exposure.
  • 5% daily loss limit — stops trading until tomorrow
  • 40% kill switch — permanent shutdown
